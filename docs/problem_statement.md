# Problem Statement

## Theme

Theme 3 — Crisis Management, HealthTech and Emergency Response

## Project

LineGuard AI — Predictive LT-Line Failure Risk Scoring

## Problem

Low-tension overhead power lines form the final electricity-distribution connection to homes, shops and communities. In rural and semi-urban areas, these line segments may be exposed to aging, corrosion, vegetation contact, repeated overloading, strong winds and long inspection intervals.

Utility teams often identify vulnerable line segments only after a conductor fails, an outage occurs or a public complaint is received. Certain broken-conductor and high-impedance conditions may not immediately produce enough overcurrent to trigger conventional protection systems.

Maintenance teams may be responsible for thousands of line segments but have limited workers, vehicles and inspection time. Without a predictive system, maintenance remains periodic, complaint-based or reactive.

## Who It Affects

- Electricity-distribution utilities
- District maintenance teams
- Field inspection workers
- Rural and semi-urban communities
- People and animals near damaged live conductors

## Why Existing Approaches Are Insufficient

Circuit breakers and protection systems mainly respond after abnormal electrical conditions develop. They do not provide a preventive ranking of which line segments are gradually becoming vulnerable.

Manual inspection of every segment is expensive and time-consuming. As a result, healthy segments may be inspected while higher-risk segments remain unattended.

## Proposed AI Approach

LineGuard AI combines line age, conductor material, visual condition, fault history, inspection interval, electrical load, overload hours, wind exposure, rainfall and vegetation proximity.

The model generates a relative risk score from 0 to 100 for every LT-line segment and classifies it as Low, Medium, High or Critical risk.

The dashboard ranks maintenance priorities, explains the strongest risk factors and recommends inspection timelines.

## Intended Impact

The system supports a shift from reactive emergency repair to preventive maintenance, helping utilities prioritise limited inspection resources before vulnerable lines become outage or electrocution hazards.
